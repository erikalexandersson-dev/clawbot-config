# Notes & study plan — Tegmark: *Consciousness as a State of Matter* (arXiv:1401.1219v3)

PDF: `docs/1401.1219v3.pdf`

## Quick orientation
- Author: Max Tegmark
- Thesis: treat consciousness as a **physical property** (“perceptronium”), analogous to a *state of matter*, characterized by information-processing principles.
- Stated principles: **Information, Integration, Independence, Dynamics**.
- Physics link: uses these principles to argue for/attack the **quantum factorization problem** (why we experience a particular Hilbert-space tensor factorization corresponding to classical space / object hierarchy).

## Paper map (high level)
- **I. Introduction**
  - Motivation: observers/measurements in physics; “quantum factorization problem”.
- **II. Integration**
  - Quantifying “integrated information” in physical systems; classical vs quantum complications.
- **III. Independence**
  - Why integration alone isn’t enough; need additional principle(s) to recover object hierarchy / locality.
- **IV. Dynamics**
  - How dynamics/time enter; connections to decoherence/Quantum Darwinism; stability criteria.
- **V. Conclusions**
  - What would count as evidence; implications; open problems.
- **Appendices**
  - Mathematical details (superoperators, separability theorem, technical proofs).

## Working glossary (to expand)
- **Hilbert space factorization**: writing the total Hilbert space as a tensor product of subsystems, \(\mathcal H = \mathcal H_A \otimes \mathcal H_B \otimes \cdots\).
- **Quantum factorization problem**: why the “subsystem split” we experience aligns with quasi-local objects in real space, not e.g. Fourier space.
- **Integrated information (Tononi/IIT)**: measures how much information is generated by the whole above and beyond its parts; Tegmark generalizes beyond neural nets.
- **Hamiltonian separability**: whether the Hamiltonian is close to a sum of terms acting on separate factors; related to object independence.
- **Hilbert–Schmidt superoperator**: a linear map acting on operators, often used to define norms/inner products on operator space.
