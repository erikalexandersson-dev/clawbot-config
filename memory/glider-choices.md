## How I’ll evaluate wings (repeatable rubric)
For each candidate:
1) **Class / concept:** EN rating, 2-liner vs 3-liner, aspect ratio (if available)
2) **Pilot profile claims:** manufacturer + independent reviewers
3) **Passive safety / behaviour in turbulence:** pitch/roll stability, collapse resistance, recovery traits
4) **Speed performance:** top speed, pressure, efficiency on bar, rear-riser control effectiveness
5) **Climb / thermalling:** bite in weak lift, turn authority, energy retention
6) **Launch & groundhandling:** nil wind, strong wind, overshoot tendency
7) **Workload / fatigue:** how “busy” it is over long XC
8) **Durability/weight:** cloth tech (DLS/ULS/light), longevity notes
9) **Operational details:** ears, spiral stability, descent techniques, trim/speed system, riser ergonomics
10) **Fit for Erik:** size/wingloading notes + what to test fly

## Collected facts (verbatim notes + links)

### Niviuk Artik 7 P — initial facts
- Niviuk markets it as **super-light and compact** ("At only 3.07 kg" is stated on the product page; likely size-dependent) and emphasizes **roll/pitch stability** + performance.
  - Source: https://niviuk.com/en/artik-7-p
- Multiple reseller/review snippets describe it as a **hybrid 3/2-liner** EN-C concept (not a full 2-liner like an “R” race-style wing).
  - (Need to pull a primary spec sheet to confirm line plan by size.)

### NOVA Mentor 7 / Mentor 7 Light — initial facts
- NOVA positions Mentor as top-end **EN-B**.
- Describes **HAC handles** on C-risers for efficient pitch control (esp. accelerated flight) and notes hybrid 2.5-liner concept does not inherently increase demands vs Mentor 6; collapses "somewhat milder" per their FAQ text.
  - Sources: https://www.nova.eu/en/gliders/mentor-7/ and https://www.nova.eu/en/gliders/mentor-7-light/

### Ozone Swift 6 vs Ozone Rush 6 — sourced differences
- XC Mag (SwiftSix review) states: “The basic wing structure of the SwiftSix is the same as the Rush 6 and it has the same hybrid 2/3-line layout; the only differences are the materials used in the wing itself and some extra porting in the cell walls.”
  - Source: https://xcmag.com/gear-guide/paraglider-reviews/ozone-swiftsix-review-en-b/
- Same source notes SwiftSix materials: primarily **Porcher Skytex 7000 27**, with leading edge **Dominico D20**.
- Same source gives an example weight comparison: SwiftSix **(ML) 4.27 kg** vs Rush 6 **(ML) 5.19 kg** (saving **920 g**).

### Advance Sigma DLS (aka Sigma 12 DLS) — early read
- Review (Dust of the Universe): describes very high comfort for a C, “close to high-B feel” in turbulence; glide competitive; full speed measured ~+11–12 km/h over trim in one test; notes 2-liners still lead in climb efficiency and top speed.
  - Source: https://dustoftheuniverse.com/2024/10/16/advance-sigma-12-dls/
- Review (Flybubble): says calmer/more pitch-stable than prior; excellent top-end among 3-liner C; “not far off best C two-liners” but best 2-liners have more speed; praises rear-riser usability/clean leading edge at full bar.
  - Source: https://flybubble.com/blog/advance-sigma-12-dls-review
- News (XC Mag): Advance positions as EN-C 3-liner “Cross Country Sportster”, “performance boost… forgiving flight characteristics”; mentions DLS + nitinol.
  - Source: https://xcmag.com/news/gear-news/advance-unveil-new-sigma-dls/
